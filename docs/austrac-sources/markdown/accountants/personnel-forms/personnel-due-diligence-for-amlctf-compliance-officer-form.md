#### Step 5: Fit and proper assessment

Assess the candidate’s suitability under the ‘fit and proper’ criteria.

If the person is a member of an accounting professional association, you have carried out a national criminal history check and you have no reasonable doubts about their integrity or identity, you can:

- assess the person’s suitability based on their capability, criminal history and any conflicts of interest
- mark all other factors as suitable.

| Assessment area | Process/instruction | Outcome | Supporting information |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Capability | An AML/CTF compliance officer doesn’t need to be an AML/CTF expert, but they must have the ability to:<br>- understand the practice's ML/TF risks<br>- apply AML/CTF policies and procedures<br>- use training and support material effectively<br>- consider the candidate’s past performance, references and training capacity. | [ ]   Suitable<br>[ ]   Not suitable |  |
| Honesty, integrity and character | Assess based on any statutory declaration, references and your professional experience with the candidate. The person should be honest, reliable and act ethically. | [ ]   Suitable<br>[ ]   Not suitable |  |
| Conflicts of interest | Assess based on any statutory declaration, references and your professional experience with the candidate. The person should not have a conflict of interest that will materially affect their ability in the role or should have sufficient controls in place to help them manage any conflict of interest. | [ ]   Suitable<br>[ ]   Not suitable |  |
| Criminal history | Review the national criminal history check and whether the person was convicted of a serious offence. Determine if any convictions affect their ability to perform AML/CTF duties. | [ ]   Suitable<br>[ ]   Not suitable |  |
