# Request to verify information

This form is to request that a conveyancer, buyer's agent or seller's agent involved in the real estate transaction verify a customer's information for initial customer due diligence. This is appropriate when verification cannot be undertaken due to time constraints. For example, verifying information about a counterparty in an auction or for more complex customers.

For the purposes of this form the entity:

- requesting verification of customer information is the 'first entity'
- that is being requested to verify information is the 'second entity'.

Other responsibilities

- The first entity must keep records of the information they have collected.
- The first entity must keep records they obtain from the second entity of the independent and reliable data used to verify information.
- These records must be kept for 7 years from either the date the transaction is completed or the date the business relationship with the customer ends.
- The first entity must keep a copy of this agreement for 7 years from either the date this transaction is completed or the date the business relationship with the customer ends.
